Yantra-Yukti

Build how things work

Take apart the everyday machines around you — a night lamp, a vending machine, a lift — map their hidden logic, then press Run and watch your design come alive.

Yantra (machine) + Yukti (logic) — kids program the invisible software of the physical world, and the app shows them exactly where their logic still needs fixing. Two more modes go further: Circuits teaches real electronics — drawn as water you can watch — and the Tinker Lab is a 3D workbench where they wire real parts by hand, then print a handout and build it for real.

How it works

No syntax to memorise — just rules, and a machine that reacts. That's the Machines mode; Circuits and the Tinker Lab below work the same way — build it, press Run, watch what happens.

1

Pick a machine

Choose an everyday machine and figure out the hidden logic that should make it work.

2

Build the rules

Assemble simple WHEN … THEN … rules from dropdowns — conditions, AND / OR / NOT, memory, timers, states. Stuck? Tap Build it with me and it walks you through a working design, one rule at a time.

3

Press Run

Watch it animate through every test. Green means it works; a red mark shows the exact moment your logic slips.

⚡ Build electronics, too

A second mode — tap 🔌 Circuits — teaches resistors, capacitors, diodes and transistors. Because electricity is invisible, it's drawn as water.

💧

Electronics as water

Voltage is water pressure, a resistor is a narrow pipe, a capacitor is a springy tank, a diode a one-way valve — physics you can actually watch flow.

🩹

Fix the broken circuit

Each puzzle hands the child a circuit that fails, and a parts bin. Fit the right part, press Run, and see it survive the surge — or pop.

🎓

Real components

The same ideas real engineers use — current limiting, smoothing, timing, one-way flow, logic gates and switching — with no maths and no code.

🔥 Current Limiter 🌗 Dimmer 💡 Surge Guard 🕯️ Steady Glow ⏳ Slow Start 🌊 Backflow Stopper 🔔 Doorbell 🎛️ Pressure Switch

🔧 New: build it in 3D, then build it for real

A third mode — tap 🔧 Tinker Lab — is an open 3D workbench stocked with real components, and more keep arriving. No puzzle to solve: build whatever you imagine.

🧲

Wire it with your hands

Drag parts onto the bench, drag wire between their terminals, and orbit around your build. Press Run and real physics takes over — bulbs brighten with power, motors speed up with current, an LED wired backwards simply won't light.

🖨️

Print it and build it

Save a design to your own catalogue, then print a handout — renders of your build from every side, a parts list, step-by-step instructions and safety notes — and make it again with real components on the kitchen table.

🧭

Never stuck

A Build with me coach walks you through your first builds step by step, Meet the parts explains what every component really does, and ready-made everyday systems — a windmill, a wet grinder, a doorbell — load and run in one tap.

🏡 For parents

Optional — set it up once, or ignore it entirely.

🧒

A profile per child

Give each child their own profile — they tap their face to jump in. Their builds, designs and progress stay their own; a sibling can't see or delete them.

⏳

A daily time limit

Choose a limit from 15 minutes to 2 hours, protected by a PIN only you know. Children see their own time ticking down and can ask for more — you can grant extra just for today.

📋

See what they built

A plain list of recent builds — which machine or circuit, how long they spent, and whether they finished it or wandered off. No scores, no leaderboards.
